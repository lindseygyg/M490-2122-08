Source: American Red Cross

Step 1: Make sure scene is safe.

Step 2: Check if person is responsive. If person is not responsive, call 911 or ask a bystander to call 911.

Step 3: Send someone to get an AED (if alone, stay with victim).

Step 4: Check for pulse and breathing. If no breathing, start CPR.

Step 5: Place hands one on top of the other with fingers interlaced in the middle of chest. Start compressions that are at least 5 cm deep and at a rate of 100 compression/per minute.

Step 6: Deliver two breaths for every thirty compressions. To do this, tilt head and chin back to open airways. Check for signs of a physical obstruction in mouth and make sure they are tilted to prevent choking. Then pinch nose and place mouth on other person’s mouth and blow until chest rises.

Step 7: Continue this process of CPR until person is breathing or shows signs of life, or until an AED or EMS arrives.

Step 8: If an AED arrives before EMS or person shows signs of life, place AED pads on victim and follow AED instructions.
