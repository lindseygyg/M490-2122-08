Pick a level to start learning or practicing CPR!

Level 1 CPR

Novice: Understanding the fundamentals of basic CPR

(includes checking the surrounding, pulse check, and compression frequency).

Level 2 CPR

Beginner: Understand how to do chest compressions properly

(includes checking the surrounding, pluse check, compression frequency, and compression depth).

Level 3 CPR

Intermediate: Understand when to perform rescue breaths

(includes checking the surrounding, pluse check, compression frequency, compression depth, head tilt, and rescue breaths).

Level 4 CPR

Advanced: Understand where to place AED pads

(includes checking the surrounding, pluse check, compression frequency, compression depth, head tilt, rescue breaths, and AED placement).

Level 5 CPR

Expert: Perform one round of CPR without guidance!

Level 5.5 CPR

Duration Expert: Perform the full CPR process three times through to test your skills.

Level 6 CPR

Timed: Like Level 5.5 but with a time element to provide additional stress to simulate a more realistic environment.

Individual Components

Need extra practice on just one part? You choose which specific components or CPR step to train further.
